How Jitsi Protects Your Data

Methods to Protect Information

Jitsi focuses on protecting data from its users, one such method revolves around its lack of use of cookies to identify users; using differing methods such as fingerprints to promote anonymity. IP addresses are not completely recorded using small octets so a completely accurate location will not be recorded utilizing its privacy mode. Geo-targeting is also another measure taken in order to comply with country policies while also placing restrictions on cookies and storing IP addresses.

As for how this pertains towards the Animal Liberation Press Office and their workings, utilizing Jitsi can create a secure form of communication between party members and their efforts with digital security. Communication as well as personal information can stay privatized against any other forms of outsiders, such as unauthorized users obtaining private information or those working against them.

User Recognition

Events from Jitsi’s anonymous users are stored with user id with authentication. The Animal Liberation Press Office has a need for this type of storage of information so as to keep confidential details private to the public, as means of operation may need to stay personal until actions are taken.

Encryption

Hop-by-hop encryption is used to protect Jitsi’s video calls, making differing parts encrypted as the meeting goes along. With Jitsu being an open-source project, any individual can investigate the code and verify issues with the project, as well as being marketed as an overall level of higher quality with lower latency for more sustainable meetings and video calls. Having a proper meeting source is needed for such a group, as for information to be given reasonably there has to be comfortability and easy access, which Jitsi would provide.
